    History

    Early Life

    Mat was born and raised in Modesto, California where he tinkered with game engines like ZZT and RPG Maker while learning to 3D model, animate, and program games at home. He had his first computer when he was 5 years old. Mat has been drawing, painting, 3D modeling, and writing since he was a child.

    US Marine Corps

    Mat enlisted in the US Marine Corps in 2007 and graduated from the Defense Language Institute, Monterey Bay with a fluency in Korean in 2009. He served 5 years in the US Marine Corps as a Korean linguist and intelligence operator, seting up and maintaining mission critical satellite communications equipment in jungle environments, facilitating medical and electronic equipment training courses for foreign military personnel and collecting intelligence in support of US military missions. Mat has traveled around the world, met, lived with and worked with a diverse range of peoples, overseen fireteams of Marines, volunteered with Toys for Tots and operated sensitive equipment in rugged and dangerous locations. He completed his military term as a sergeant in 2011.


    College

    Mat's next adventure began immediately after, at the Academy of Art University in San Francisco, studying Illustration and 3D Modeling. Mat eventually transferred to CSU Monterey Bay and attained a BS in Computer Science with a concentration in Game Programming in 2018. During his time at CSUMB, Mat worked as an undergraduate researcher and developed the Gauntlet VR travel technique, about which he wrote and published a paper at IEEE VR 2017 and also lectured a few classes on setting up and using Oculus Rift headsets and Leap Motion optical hand tracking devices. During Mat's final semester at CSUMB, he taught a game development class to highschool students at Everette Alvarez Highschool utilizing PlayCanvas (a Chromebook friendly, browser-based Javascript game engine similar to Unity 3D). For that course, he wrote 36 pages of Javascript, PlayCanvas and generic game design documentation for the students. Simultaneously, Mat worked closely with a game designer and two programmers to design game mechanics and develop an educational, underwater VR game called Open Ocean VR in Unity3D. Mat was responsible for 3D modeling each of the marine wildlife in Blender, programming their movement behaviors and the game's event system as well as creating Unity 3D tools for level designers to utilize.


    Indie Game Developer

    During that time, Mat worked as an independent video game developer, designing and developing experimental game projects, like Fleet and Combat Descent, and a few other projects that were released on the iOS App Store, like Retro Remix and Attack Run Alpha.


    Family

    During his time in college, Mat met and married his wife. They've been married for 9 years and have a child and three cats.


    HaptX and Tapestry Solutions

    Mat had a start at HaptX in 2019, where, in his short time, he created a haptic VR design tool in Unreal Engine that could be used to review automotive or industrial designs in real-time. From there, Mat moved on to work at Tapestry Solutions, where he worked for 3 years developing government and commercial logistics software used to automate ship and warehouse loading. Mat did front-end work in React, Redux, and TypeScript and also back-end work on the REST API, SQL database, and Kafka messaging queues.


    Return to HaptX

    Mat returned to HaptX in 2022 and began work designing a VR demo set aboard the International Space Station, where the user remotely piloted a robot and used HaptX haptic gloves and Airpack to feel what the robot felt while fixing the space station. Next, Mat and the content team began designing and developing the new G1 Airpack and Glove's VR haptic demo. The demo includes 4 scenes, each of which demonstrates various features of the gloves or types of training scenarios that a developer could create with the gloves. Mat created 3 of the 4 scenes, including the the gameplay programming, environments, level layouts, materials, textures, SFX, lighting, haptic effects, and a majority of the 3D models. The G1 Demo won Best in Show - Audience Choice at AWE 2024. During his time with HaptX, Mat gave hundreds of demos, tested hundreds of gloves and Airpacks, created new software and processes to validate glove functionality, directly improved the Unreal Engine plugin, and lectured to a college class about VR and haptics. Mat's deep knowledge and experience as the team member with the most time wearing the G1 gloves was widely used to ensure good investor and conference demos were given and that gloves arriving from the manufacturer were functioning as intended.
